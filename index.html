The GPS precedent

We have seen this before.
One function. Measurable damage.

In 2017, UCL researchers found that London taxi drivers who switched from memory-based navigation to GPS experienced measurable shrinkage of the posterior hippocampus — the brain region for spatial memory.

The technology worked perfectly. The brain adapted by discarding what it no longer needed.

GPS offloaded one cognitive function. AI offloads dozens — simultaneously. Writing, analysis, reasoning, decision-making. The entire upper cortex.

The erosion is invisible because the tool causing it is always there to compensate. You only discover the damage when the AI is unavailable.
