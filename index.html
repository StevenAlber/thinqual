The GPS precedent

We have seen this before.
One function. Measurable damage.

In 2017, UCL researchers found that London taxi drivers who switched to GPS experienced measurable shrinkage of the posterior hippocampus — the brain region for spatial memory.

The technology worked perfectly. The brain adapted by discarding what it no longer needed.

GPS offloaded one cognitive function. AI offloads dozens — simultaneously. Writing, analysis, reasoning, decision-making. The entire upper cortex.

The erosion is invisible because the tool causing it is always there to compensate. You discover the damage only when the AI is unavailable — and by then it's already significant.
